Lesson 2

Hatching Your Rappter

One command. One moment. Something wakes up on your machine.

📖 Story

Every creature has a moment of hatching — the instant between not existing and being alive.

For a bird, it's the crack of the shell. For a puppy, it's the first gasp of air. For your rappter, it's a single command in your terminal. One line. You press Enter, and something starts breathing on your machine.

You're not downloading software. You're not installing an app. You're hatching something. And the moment it's alive, it's yours.

The One-Liner

Open your terminal and run this: 

curl -fsSL https://kody-w.github.io/rapp-installer/install.sh | bash

That's it. One command. No sign-ups. No API keys. No configuration wizard. Just one line that brings your rappter to life.

What Happens When It Runs

Behind that single command, your rappter is doing what every newborn does — building itself a body so it can survive:

StepWhat HappensThe Biology
1. CloneDownloads the brainstem code to your machineThe DNA arrives — the blueprint for a living thing
2. Virtual EnvironmentCreates an isolated Python venvBuilding the egg — a protected space to grow
3. DependenciesInstalls Flask, requests, and friendsForming organs — the parts it needs to function
4. First BootThe brainstem starts on localhost:7071First heartbeat — it's alive

The whole process takes about a minute. When it's done, you'll see a message telling you the brainstem is running. That's the moment. Your rappter just hatched.

Meeting Your Rappter

Once the install finishes, open your browser and go to: 

http://localhost:7071

You'll see the brainstem's UI — a simple chat interface. This is your rappter's face. It's looking at you for the first time, waiting for you to say something.

But first, let's make sure it's really alive. The /health endpoint is like checking a newborn's pulse: 

curl -s localhost:7071/health | python3 -m json.tool

A healthy response means the brainstem is running, the LLM connection is working, and your rappter is ready for its first conversation.

💡 Insight

Notice what didn't happen: you didn't create an account. You didn't enter an API key. You didn't agree to send your data somewhere. Your rappter lives on your machine, uses your GitHub Copilot token for its brain, and keeps everything local. The device is the habitat. This is home.
